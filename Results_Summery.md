**Reference:** DBA-JWLG-2026  
**Prepared by:** Satwik Kamlakar Udupi

## Executive Summary

This analysis presents the Dam Break Analysis (DBA) and Standard Project Flood (SPF) hydrograph study for **Jawalgaon Dam**, a medium irrigation project in Barshi Taluk, Solapur District, Maharashtra, India. The study is conducted to support Emergency Action Planning (EAP) and dam safety compliance under the **Dam Safety Act, 2021**.

## EAP Implications

1. **OVTP Scenario:** Peak downstream discharge of 1,838 m³/s provides the design flood for downstream inundation mapping. The flood wave travel time (upstream breach → downstream peak) defines the **minimum EAP notification time** for downstream communities.

2. **PIPG Scenario:** Piping failure at a constant 7.0 m³/s represents a lower but potentially earlier-warning scenario. The insidious nature of piping requires heightened routine inspection under normal reservoir operations.

3. **Reservoir Levels:** The EAC curves directly inform spillway operation and reservoir flood routing for real-time EAP implementation.
